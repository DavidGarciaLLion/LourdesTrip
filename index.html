The plan at a glance

Built around your four must-do climbs — Aubisque/Soulor, Luz Ardiden, Tourmalet, Hautacam — alternating hard and lighter days, never stacking the two hardest (Tourmalet & Hautacam) together, and keeping Sunday short for your afternoon drive home.

⚠️ This is a maximal block — four hard days, no recovery. Adding Aspin to Saturday (which, with no shuttle, means climbing the Tourmalet from both sides) and moving Hautacam to Friday means every day is now hard: ~10 900 m of climbing over 4 consecutive days, finishing with a high-altitude queen stage on your travel day. This is a big ask even for 3–4 W/kg riders. Levers to dial back if needed: keep Friday as a recovery day (do Luz Ardiden only and drop Hautacam), drop Bordères on Thursday, and/or take Day 4 Option B (Col de Tentes only) for an earlier finish before the drive home.

How the days break down

  • Day 2 (Fri) — Luz Ardiden + Hautacam, two HC climbs ridden from the door (~82 km / 2 300 m).
  • Day 3 (Sat) — the queen stage: Tourmalet from both sides with Col d'Aspin in the middle (~96 km / 3 250 m), the only no-shuttle way to ride Aspin and get home. Drive 20 min to Luz to start.
  • Day 4 (Sun) — high-altitude finale: Cirque de Troumouse (2 089 m) + Col de Tentes (2 208 m), both higher than the Tourmalet.
  • This knowingly breaks your original "2 hard / 2 easy" and "easy travel day" rules — the direct consequence of packing in Aspin, Hautacam and the high cols. Flagged so it's your informed call.
No support vehicle needed: every route rides from the door or a short drive to Luz — no shuttles or point-to-point.
Trim to 9 climbs / add a rest: on Friday, ride Luz Ardiden only (Option B) and drop Hautacam — that restores one recovery day before the big weekend.

Day 1 · Thursday — HARD Aubisque · Soulor · Spandelles

The queen stage, done on fresh legs. Two big passes linked by the spectacular Cirque du Litor balcony road, with a wild, narrow finale over the Col de Spandelles — the exact climb that decided the 2022 Tour de France stage into Hautacam.

Option A RECOMMENDED — Bordères → Soulor → Aubisque → Spandelles

Ride from the hotel — no driving needed. A quick leg-opener over Col des Bordères near Arrens makes this your 8th climb without touching the short Sunday.

Route: Argelès → Arrens-Marsous → warm-up over Col des Bordères (1 156 m) → back to Arrens → Col du Soulor (1 474 m) → balcony to Col d'Aubisque (1 709 m) → back over Soulor → descend to Ferrières → Col de Spandelles (1 378 m) → descend to Argelès.

Leaner alternative: skip Bordères for a 90 km / 2 550 m / ~4h45 day with 3 climbs (→ 7 climbs for the trip).

Option B EASIER ALT — Aubisque out-and-back via Col des Bordères

Skip the steep, rough Spandelles; return the way you came and add the little Col des Bordères. ~80 km / 2 050 m / ~4h00 / 3 climbs. Choose this if the weather's marginal (Spandelles is exposed and unmaintained).

Highlights along the way

  • Cirque du Litor balcony — the Soulor→Aubisque road is carved into the cliff above the Ouzoum ravine, passing under a natural stone arch and through a 100 m tunnel. One of the most photographed roads in the Pyrenees.
  • Col d'Aubisque — giant painted bicycle sculptures at the summit; sweeping views to the Pic du Midi d'Ossau. A Tour de France fixture since 1910.
  • Arrens-Marsous — pretty Lavedan village, gateway to the Néouvielle nature reserve; good fountains to top up bottles.
  • Col de Spandelles — silent, single-lane forest road with grazing sheep; the descent's hairpins are where Vingegaard waited for a crashed Pogačar in 2022.

Suggested breaks

☕ Short — 15 min · Arrens-Marsous (~km 15) — last village before the Soulor ramp; fountain + café, fill bottles for the long climb.
🍽️ Long — 30 min · Col d'Aubisque summit (~km 45) — the famous summit café/buvette. Refuel before retracing the balcony; put on your jacket for the descent.

Two HC summit finishes in a day — Luz Ardiden's famous hairpins, then the savage wall of Hautacam back above Argelès. Ridden straight from the door; the valley between the two valleys is your transfer and recovery spin.

Highlights along the way

  • Luz Ardiden — a staircase of perfectly engineered hairpins above the treeline; site of Indurain's and Armstrong's legendary battles. The top opens onto a wide ski-bowl panorama.
  • Luz-Saint-Sauveur — fortified 12th-century Templar church (Église des Templiers) and Napoleon III's spa-era "Pont Napoléon" bridge over a dramatic gorge.
  • Hautacam — irregular, punchy ramps with zero warm-up; Tour-decisive in 1996, 2008, 2014 and 2022, with a panoramic balcony over the whole Argelès valley.
  • Ayros-Arbouix & the Lavedan hamlets — slate-roofed farmhouses and chapels on Hautacam's lower slopes.

Suggested breaks

☕ Short — 15 min · Luz-Saint-Sauveur (after descending Luz Ardiden) — café in the old town before spinning back to Argelès.
🍽️ Long — 30 min · Argelès-Gazost (between the two climbs) — refuel in town before taking on Hautacam's brutal opening ramps.

Day 3 · Saturday — HARD Tourmalet (×2) + Col d'Aspin

The queen stage. With no support vehicle, the only way to ride Aspin and get home is to cross the Tourmalet, climb Aspin from Sainte-Marie-de-Campan, then climb the Tourmalet back the other way — three iconic ascents in one big mountain day. Drive 20 min to Luz to start at the foot of the climb.

Option A RECOMMENDED — Tourmalet (W) → Aspin → Tourmalet (E)

Drive Argelès → Luz-Saint-Sauveur (~20 min), then the classic out-and-back over the Géant with Aspin in the belly of the loop.

Highlights along the way

  • Col du Tourmalet — the "Géant du Tourmalet" statue (Octave Lapize) at 2 115 m; first climbed by the Tour in 1910. The final 4 km at 9–10% are the sting. You'll earn it twice today.
  • Pont de la Gaubie / Voie Laurent Fignon — a traffic-free cyclists-only old-road section partway up the west side.
  • Sainte-Marie-de-Campan — the valley village at the foot of both the Tourmalet (east) and Aspin; good services and your turnaround base.
  • Col d'Aspin — gentler, greener col with grazing cattle and horses and a postcard panorama back to the Tourmalet massif — a beautiful contrast in the middle of a brutal day.

Suggested breaks

☕ Short — 15 min · Col d'Aspin summit (~km 48) — the day's high-point of calm; soak up the view before turning back toward the Tourmalet.
🍽️ Long — 30 min · Sainte-Marie-de-Campan (~km 60) — proper refuel in the valley before the second, east-side ascent of the Tourmalet — the crux of the day.

Day 4 · Sunday — HARD Cirque de Troumouse + Col de Tentes

A high-altitude queen stage to finish — two summits higher than the Tourmalet (2 089 m & 2 208 m), deep in the UNESCO Gavarnie–Mont-Perdu world heritage area. Drive 20 min to Luz, then climb into the most spectacular high cirques in the range.

This is now your travel day. It's a big mountain day (~4h40 riding + stops) finishing high and cold. Start early (07:30–08:00); if your drive home is long, take Option B (Col de Tentes only) to be back by lunch.

Save ~1 h on the travel day: drive all the way to Gèdre (~30 min) to skip the Luz–Gèdre valley (~66 km / 2 200 m).

Option B SHORTER — for a long drive home — Col de Tentes only

Drop Troumouse and ride only the higher, more iconic summit: Luz → Gavarnie → Col de Tentes (2 208 m) → return. ~58 km / ~1 525 m / ~3h00 — still a 2 200 m+ finish, back in time for an early-afternoon getaway.

Altimetry profiles

Highlights along the way

  • Cirque de Troumouse — a vast glacial amphitheatre ringed by 3 000 m peaks; a narrow, quiet, bumpy road with spectacular hairpins and a Virgin statue at the top. Far less travelled than its famous neighbours.
  • Gèdre & the Gavarnie valley — UNESCO World Heritage; slate villages and deep gorge scenery.
  • Gavarnie — gateway to the legendary Cirque de Gavarnie and its 400 m waterfall (a short walk if time allows); lively village with cafés.
  • Col de Tentes — higher than the Tourmalet, on the Spanish border, staring straight at the Brèche de Roland and the Vignemale glacier. A wild, almost traffic-free dead-end col — a spectacular finale.

Suggested breaks

☕ Short — 15 min · Gèdre (between the two climbs) — refuel in the village before heading up to Gavarnie and Tentes.
🍽️ Long — 30 min · Gavarnie — last services; eat well and add all your layers before the cold, exposed final climb to Col de Tentes.

Logistics & tips

  • Driving distances from Argelès: Luz-Saint-Sauveur ~20 min · Aucun/Arrens ~20 min · Pierrefitte ~10 min. All comfortably inside your 30–40 min limit.
  • Bike shops / rescue: shops in Argelès and Luz; the Tourmalet, Aubisque and Hautacam summits have buvettes in season. Last services on Day 4 are at Gavarnie/Gèdre — nothing on the high cols.
  • Road opening: all cols here are open in September (Spandelles & Aubisque close ~Nov–Apr; Tentes & Troumouse close with the first snows, usually late Oct). Bikes ride the Troumouse toll free and may pass its top boom gate.
  • Fuelling: carry 2 bottles + food on Days 1, 3 and 4 — long service gaps above Barèges, on Spandelles, and on the Troumouse/Tentes high roads.
